Schedule and Budget

Schedule


The development of T3MPO was a two semester, or academic year, long project. In the first term, Spring 2013, our design project was selected and researched. The necessary hardware and software stacks were investigated and the development environment was setup on each team member's computer. In the second term, Fall 2013, the implementation of T3MPO was undertaken under the guidance of our faculty advisor, Dr. Tom Holton. The schedule is summarized in the table below:


Date (2013) Tasks
February-March
  • Team Assembled
  • Project selected
  • Research started
April-May
  • Gathered necessary hardware and software resources.
  • Researched use cases
June-August
  • Set-up development environment on each team member's computer
  • Studied further source code of OpenNI and Synapse
  • Began learning to use Ableton Live
September
  • Started development in two tiers: primary and alternative
  • Determined how to parse hand-position data
  • Wrote algorithm to detect button pushes
October
  • Finished alternative, or standalone, application
  • Programmed graphics for button grid
November
  • Finished standalone application
  • Developed MPC Max for Live module
  • Established custom UDP communication between Synapse and Max/MSP modules
December
  • Satisfied primary T3MPO design requirements
  • Programmed additonal T3MPO modules: scratcher, guitar, and synapse modes
  • Performed quality assurance testing
Table 1. Schedule for the development of T3MPO

Budget


To develop T3MPO, the necessary hardware and software had to be acquired. Our team actually had all the resources required prior to development and did not have to spend much money. However, if no resources were available prior to development, the monetary costs incurred would have been as follows:


Component Cost ($)
Computer 500-1000
Kinect 100
Ableton Live 100
Max for Live 200
OpenNI Free
Synapse Free
Total 900-1400
Table 2. Breakdown of costs to develop T3MPO from scratch

Our team already had much of the required resources already gathered. Everyone on the team had a computer, a team member was already in possession of a Kinect sensor. A couple team members are involved with music production and already had Ableton Live with Max for Live installed on their machines. Thus the actual cost for our team to develop T3MPO was the purchase of two refurbished Kinect sensors, which totaled $120.
